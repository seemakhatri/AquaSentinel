The project

What AquaSentinel does

Uses real Atal Bhujal Yojana government data to train a Random Forest classifier that predicts whether a district is Safe, Critical, or Over-Exploited — before the crisis hits.

Key findings

Numbers that matter

63m
Jaipur's water table is 63 metres underground — the deepest of the three districts studied and classified as Over-Exploited by CGWB.
−3.4m
Ajmer's water table dropped 3.4 metres in just 4 years (2019–2023). If this rate continues, it crosses the critical threshold by 2028.
80%
The model predicts district risk at 80.4% accuracy using only 4 engineered features — without needing population data or crop records.
350
350 real groundwater monitoring wells from the Government of India Atal Bhujal Yojana dataset — no synthetic data.
